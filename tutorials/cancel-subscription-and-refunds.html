Canceling Your Subscription + Refund Questions

A clear, low-stress workflow for cancel requests, billing confusion, and what to include so support can resolve it faster.

If you're feeling "this is over my head", you're not alone. This guide helps you cancel cleanly and ask for billing help without getting stuck in back-and-forth.

Step 1: Cancel from the account page first

  1. Open heyron.ai/account.
  2. Cancel your active subscription there.
  3. Take a screenshot of the final confirmation screen.

That screenshot is your proof if anything looks off later.

Step 2: Set realistic refund timing expectations

  • Cancellation and refund processing are separate steps.
  • Even after support confirms action, card processors/banks can delay posting.
  • A pending status right after cancel is common and not automatically an error.

Important: pro-rated refunds are policy-dependent. Ask clearly, but don’t assume all plans auto-prorate partial-day or partial-cycle usage.

Step 3: Send a support packet that can be actioned immediately

Billing support request Signup/billing email: Cancellation timestamp (with timezone): What you expected (cancel only / refund request / prorated request): What happened instead: Confirmation screenshot available? (yes/no): Any transaction ID or last 4 digits (optional):

If Whambulance says it can’t DM you

  1. Enable Discord DMs from server members.
  2. Post one fresh support message (avoid sending multiple duplicates).
  3. If DM still fails, include your billing email directly in #help so support can pick it up.

High-friction edge cases

  • Wrong email mismatch: you canceled on one account but requested refund help from a different email.
  • Duplicate tickets: multiple posts can cause confusion about current status. Keep one canonical thread/message.
  • Same-day signup + cancel: this often needs manual review rather than instant automated resolution.
  • Support marked resolved too early: repost with the exact open issue + your original cancellation timestamp.

When to escalate again

  • Your account still shows active after cancellation confirmation.
  • A new charge appears after confirmed cancel time.
  • You were told it was resolved, but status is unchanged after reasonable processing time.

Bottom line: cancel first, capture proof, then submit one clean billing packet. That shortens resolution time and avoids support loops.

This guide is about process clarity, not policy guarantees. Final billing/refund outcomes depend on current heyron billing policy and payment processor handling times.
